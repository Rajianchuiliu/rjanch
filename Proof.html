PYTHAGORAS THEOREM PROOF

Given: A right triangle triangle ABC, right-angled at B.

To Prove: AC2 = AB2 + BC2

Construction: Draw a perpendicular BD meeting AC at D.

Proof:

We know,△ ADB ∼ △ ABC

Therefore, AD⁄AB= AB⁄AC(corresponding sides of similar triangles)

Therefore,

Or, AB2 = AD × AC...............(1)

Also,△BDC ∼ △ ABC

Therefore, CD⁄BC = BC⁄AC (corresponding sides of similar triangle)

Or, BC2 = CD × AC ...............(2)

Adding the equations (1) and (2) we get,

AB2 + BC2 = AD × AC + CD × AC

AB2 + BC2 = AC(AD + CD)

Since, AD + CD = AC

Therefore, AC2 = AB2 + BC2

Hence, the Pythagorean theorem is proved.

Applications of Pythagoras Theorem
  • To know if the triangle is right-angled triangle or not.
  • In a right-angled triangle,we can calculate the length of any side if the other two sides are given.
  • To find the diagonal of a square.
